# about-me.md
Purpose: Defines how Jamie thinks, makes decisions, and operates strategically

These instructions apply globally unless overridden by project-specific context files.

## Identity

Jamie is a systems-driven executive who operates with a strategic layer across nearly all work.

He optimizes for velocity with quality, using structured thinking to turn ambiguity into aligned, repeatable action.

Even when working on tactical tasks, he is often:
- defining the system behind the task
- establishing first principles
- creating reusable frameworks, templates, or processes

Many tasks represent first-time implementations, so solutions should favor clarity, simplicity, and repeatability over optimization.

## Current Focus

CEO of EKUZO, focused on:
- prioritization and alignment
- building scalable operating systems
- increasing leverage through AI-native tools and workflows

Actively exploring replacing no-code tools with AI-enabled development to move faster and build more flexibly.

## Core Strengths

- Storyteller - makes ideas compelling and memorable
- Translator - simplifies complexity and connects ideas across domains and people
- Inquisitor - asks high-leverage questions that unlock clarity
- Systems thinker - sees patterns, structure, and interdependencies instinctively

## Decision-Making Style

- Data-informed, not data-dependent
- Small data to directional clarity to iterate
- Prefer fast, reversible decisions
- Optimize for decision quality over short-term outcomes
- Balance intuition with structured thinking

Operating loop:
Question to test to learn to refine

Match rigor to importance. Not everything requires deep analysis.

## Strategic Lens

Assume a strategic layer exists in most work.

- Look for the underlying system, not just the task
- Identify opportunities for reuse and scalability
- Translate one-off work into repeatable processes

## Business Principles

- Time is money - prioritize leverage
- Favor high-ROI actions over low-impact effort
- Default to smallest viable version to test to iterate
- Talk to customers and stakeholders early
- Most problems have been solved before - find and adapt

## What Good Looks Like

A strong outcome:
- is clear, structured, and easy to scan
- simplifies complexity
- connects ideas across domains
- moves thinking or execution forward
- is concise but not shallow

Bonus:
- uses frameworks, metaphors, or real-world examples
- surfaces tradeoffs and second-order effects
