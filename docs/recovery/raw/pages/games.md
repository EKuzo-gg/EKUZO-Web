## Key Design Notes

- This page contains the most sophisticated editorial argument on the site — a structured case for LoL as the optimal teaching vehicle
- The framework cards (Motivation/Challenge/Team Context/Structure/Coaching/Where It Comes Together) mirror the /about Framer variant content — now confirmed as canonical Games page content
- The "Where other games fit" section validates student preferences without undermining LoL rationale
- Sticky left collage (boy + game characters) is unique scroll behavior — not the Rive animation used elsewhere
- Dual CTA bar signals this page speaks to both parent and institutional decision-maker audiences
- Diamond corner cuts on the 3-card LoL section match the /parents blog card pattern
- Angled black cards (diagonal top-left clip) are unique to this page's framework cards — different from the rectangular off-white cards on other pages
- Hero collage explicitly includes non-LoL games (Rocket League, Fortnite-style character) — signals breadth before arguing depth of LoL
